Energy in Europe

a visualization by Kenneth Vun Jun Zhe

The depletion of many vital non-renewable sources of energy, especially fossil fuel, has been a topic of discussion for many of those with interest or knowledge in the field of energy.

Europe is a continent where many countries are leading the way in using renewable resources more and more to slowly replace the depleting resources.

This visualization aims to reveal Europe's progress in replacing the depleting non-renewable sources of energy with renewable ones.

The Energy Powerhouse
of Europe

This first visualization illustrates the total amount of "Generation" (Power Generation) from each European country for each year from 2000 to 2020.

It is safe to say that Germany is the largest producer of power in Europe throughout 21 years from 2000 to 2020. In contrast, Luxembourg is the smallest producer.

The contribution of various energy sources in generating power from each country can be observed through this visualization as well.

Share of Production &
Change from Last Year

This Scattered Plot Chart shows the relationship between Share of Production (%) and Generation(TWh) for each country, additionally categorized by Energy Source. It also depicts the changes compared to last year(%).

One of many that can be observed is that French is the country that generates the most power through Nuclear. Another one would be that Germany produces the most power through Fossil fuels.

The Energy Source
Europe Is Most Reliant ON

This visualization exhibits the total generation of power from each energy source, throughout all of Europe from 2000 to 2020.

It can be concluded, unsurprisingly, that Fossil fuel is the source Europe generates their power from. It is closely followed by Nuclear and Coal.

Renewable vs Non-Renewable Energy Sources

The 2 map visualizations above highlight total share of production of energy from renewable sources, and from non-renewable sources respectively.

It can be established that every country used both sources in varying ratios throughout the years from 2000 to 2020. However, it can be seen that in ther latter years, the reliance renewable sources has seen a slow yet steady increase and vice versa, the reliance on non-renewable sources is on a very slight decline.

What Can Be Established?

These visualizations seem to share a mutual finding, and that is the trend where reliance on renewable resources is seeing an increase, albeit slow.

As a result, the reliance on non-renewable resources is also seeing a slight downward trend.

This is very good news, especially when many of These non-renewable resources have had people predicting the day they are depleted. These renewable resources are proving to be an excellent alternative.
